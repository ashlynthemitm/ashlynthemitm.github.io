Bio

I am a PhD student in the School of Information at the University of Michigan, mentored by Barbara Ericson and Mark Guzdial, where I study the design of community-centered Computing Education technologies through the lens of Human-Computer Interaction and Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence. My research focuses on how knowledge is produced, circulated, and legitimized across online discussions and peer networks—particularly in learning communities and sociotechnical systems.

I am a human-centered artificial intelligence researcher interested in improving the design of learning communities online. I earned my BS in Computer Science and Sociology from Georgia State University, where my undergraduate research centered on near-peer mentorship models and educational applications of natural language processing and machine learning.
